Dr. Francis Collins, M.D., Ph.D.

The Physician-Geneticist who cracked the DNA code

Here's a timeline of Dr. Collin's life:

  • 1950 - Born in Shenandoah Valley, Virginia on a small farm.
  • 1970 - Graduated from University of Virginia with a B.S. Chemistry degree.
  • 1974 - Graduated from Yale University as a Doctor of Philosophy in Physical Chemistry.
  • 1977 - Collins changed careers, earning a Doctor of Medicine degree from University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill and served residency and chief residency.
  • 1981 - Returned to Yale as a Fellow in Human Genetics
  • 1984 - Published a paper with Sherman Weissman regarding "chromosome jumping".
  • 1984 - Collins later became a professor in internal medicine and human genetics at University of Michigan, which developed his curiousity for our modern-day molecular genetics, "positional cloning".
  • 1985-1989 - Collins and his team identified the locus for the gene they've been searching for at Toronto's Hospital for Sick Children, which was later published in the journal Science.
  • 1993 - Collins became the director of the National Center for today's National Human Genome Research Institute (NHGRI).
  • 1994 - Collins founded NHGRI's Division of Intramural Research.
  • 2000 - President Bill Clinton, Biologist Craig Venter, and Francis Collins pubclicly announced the drafting of the human genome.
  • 2000 - Shared A&E Network's title "Biography of the Year" with J. Craig Venter.
  • 2003 - Collins and other scientists officially finished and published the human genome sequence (reference version).
  • 2006 - Published his book, "The Language of God: A Scientist Presents Evidence for Belief".
  • 2007 - Founded the BioLogos Foundation as President for the existence of science and faith.
  • 2008 - Resigned from NHGRI as Director, but also continued his research lab specifically towards Type 2 Diabetes and Progeria.
  • 2009 - Nominated as Director of the National Institutes of Health by President Barack Obama.
  • 2010 - Received the Albany Medical Center Prize
  • 2011 - Helped establish the National Center for Advancing Translational Sciences (NCATS).
  • 2012 - Commenced research towards the support of Alzheimer's Disease.
  • 2012 - Commenced research towards the Brain Research via Advancing Innovative Neurotechnologies (BRAIN).
  • 2013 - Assisted research with the newly partnership between NIH and the Accelerating Medicines Partnership (AMP) consisting of the U.S. Food and Drug Administration, multiple non-profit organizations, and 10 biopharmaceutical firms.
  • 2013 - As part of the NCATS, Collins created scientific roles on diversity in the scientic workforce and on Big Data.
  • 2015 - As Director of NIH, Collins announced that they will discontinue biomedical research that involves chimpanzees.
  • 2016 - President Obama announced an provided a $680 million increase towards NIH's budget for cancer research.
  • 2017 - President Donald Trump announced his consent for Collins to continue as Director of NIH.
  • 2018 - Created NIH's Helping to End Addiction Long-term (HEAL) Initiative for the improvements of opiod addiction and the enhacement of pain management.
  • 2018 - Addressed sexual harassment and the negative culture towards women and underrepresented groups in science.

Dr. Collin's Awards:

  • 1993 - The Kilby International Award
  • 2001 - The Biotechnology Heritage Award alongside J. Craig Venter
  • 2005 - The William Allan Award from American Society of Human Genetics
  • 2005 - Collins and Venter were both awarded as "America's Best Leaders" by Harvard University Center for Public Leadership and U.S. News & World Report
  • 2007 - The Presidential Medal of Freedom
  • 2008 - The Inamori Ethics Prize, the National Medal of Science, and the Trotter Prize for his lecture, "The Language of God"
  • 2012 - Pro Bono Humanum Award from the Galien Foundation
  • 2017 - The Federation of American Societies for Experimental Biology (FASEB)
  • 2018 - The Potential Key Scientific Award and the Warren Alpert Foundation Prize
